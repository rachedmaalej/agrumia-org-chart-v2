How to read these

Figures are gross, before the employee's CNSS (~9.18%) and progressive income tax — net lands around 75–85% of gross at these levels. On top of gross, budget ~17% employer CNSS plus minor levies. Transport and meal allowances, and a 13th-month bonus, are widespread and sit on top.

For context

Tunisia's minimum wage (SMIG) is 528 TND/month (48h, 2025–26); the national average gross is roughly 1,200–1,570 TND. Hospitality is seasonal, so consider core + seasonal contracts. For key roles, pitching base slightly above market aids retention given the quiet-luxury positioning.

Incentives — bringing in big-event revenue

Three programs for event-driven revenue

Hospitality best practice: split reward into a secure base plus a variable tied to results; pay commission on margin, never the headline price; blend individual and team metrics; keep the maths transparent and audited; and pay promptly on collected revenue. Below are three distinct programs targeting the people who bring in weddings, team-buildings and large events — run alone or layered.

ADirect event-sales commission

Rewards the person who sources and signs each new big event.

TargetsResponsable Marketing & Commercial (primary); the coordinator for inbound conversions.
How it worksA commission on the event's gross margin — not the headline price — so you never pay on pass-through F&B cost or taxes. Indicative: ~8–12% of margin (or ~5% of net contract value), tiered so cumulative quarterly volume unlocks a higher rate. New business only.
GuardrailsPaid after the client has settled in full; commission on margin to protect profit; no payout if discounting breaches a floor; documented as taxable pay (CNSS / IRPP).
Best forGiving the commercial role direct line-of-sight between effort and earnings.
Trade-offIndividual focus can sideline the delivery team and tempt over-discounting to close.
BEvent profit-share pool

Rewards everyone who delivered the event, not just who sold it.

TargetsThe full event team — commercial lead, coordinator, kitchen / F&B, service, housekeeping.
How it worksFund a pool with a fixed slice of each qualifying event's gross margin (~3–5%), distributed by weighting — a larger share to the seller and the on-site event lead, smaller shares across delivery roles. Keep the department payout fixed but give top contributors more.
GuardrailsOnly events above a set size; full payout unlocks only if the event clears a guest-satisfaction / review threshold — tying reward to quality, not just the sale. Self-funded from margin; quarterly payout; transparent tracking.
Best forMaking the whole event machine pull together, protecting delivery quality, and aiding cross-department retention.
Trade-offDilutes the individual seller's incentive; needs clean margin accounting and fair weighting.
C"Apporteur d'affaires" referral bounty

Rewards any employee who brings a lead that converts — no need to close it.

TargetsEvery employee (gardener, housekeeper, server, front desk) — and optionally trusted external partners.
How it worksA flat finder's fee per converted big event — e.g. ~200–500 TND for a signed-and-paid wedding or team-building, scaled to size (or a small 1–2%). The referrer registers the lead; the commercial lead closes it.
GuardrailsLead must be net-new and logged before it's otherwise in the pipeline (first-to-register avoids disputes); paid on signed-and-collected contract; simple lead log; documented for tax.
Best forCheaply widening the funnel and leveraging staff's local word-of-mouth — culturally powerful for Tunisian weddings, where personal networks drive bookings. Every role can earn.
Trade-offSmaller per-event motivation; needs disciplined lead tracking; doesn't reward closing or delivery skill.

Recommended for Agrumia

Layer them rather than choose one. At launch, run C (referral bounty for all staff) alongside A (commission for the Marketing & Commercial lead) — cheap, inclusive, and tied directly to the role whose job is revenue. As event volume builds, add B (a modest team pool behind a quality gate) so the delivery team shares the upside and stays aligned. Across all three: pay on collected revenue, commission on margin rather than headline price, keep the calculation transparent and audited (not a shared spreadsheet), and document every payout for CNSS / IRPP through the siège.

Best-practice sources: Mews, Oaky, Canary Technologies, Frontline Performance Group, Emersion Wellness, Tripleseat, LOVU.
